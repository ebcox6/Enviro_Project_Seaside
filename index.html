Environmental Project: Biomimicry and Children's Book

Welcome to the Environmental Project website! This project focuses on understanding local and global environmental issues, applying biomimicry as an innovative solution, and creating a children's book to educate others about these environmental challenges.

Project Overview

Students will work through a series of workshops, learning about local and global environmental issues, comparing different solutions, and using nature as inspiration to create innovative solutions. The final product will be a children's book, designed to teach young readers (2nd grade level or below) about environmental problems and how nature can inspire solutions.

Workshops

  1. Research and Analysis (Weeks 1-2): Students research local and global environmental issues, document their findings, and compare different contexts.
  2. Comparative Analysis and Storyboarding (Weeks 2-4): Students use Venn diagrams to compare local and global issues and create storyboards for their children's book.
  3. Biomimicry Integration (Week 5): Students learn about biomimicry and integrate a nature-inspired solution into their story.
  4. Book Development (Weeks 6-7): Students create their final product, either digitally or physically, with illustrations and engaging storytelling.
  5. Final Presentation and Reflection (Week 7): Students present their book to a 2nd-grade audience and reflect on their learning process.

Grading and Evaluation

Students will be assessed based on their participation in workshops, creativity, understanding of biomimicry, group collaboration, and the quality of their final product. A detailed rubric is provided for each workshop and the final presentation.
